Basic Ingredients

When creating a stir fry, creativity is key. Stir Fry's can be a vessel for you to flex your culinary expertise, or an avenue for you to get rid of fresh produce sitting in your fridge or countertop. Saying that, there are basic ingredients to every stir fry that you have to use.

  • Protein (chicken, beef, pork, or tofu)
  • Aromatics (garlic, onions, cloves, spices, ect)
  • Oil (olive oil, butter, ghee, avocado oil, ect.)
  • Veggies (brocoli, carrots, onions, mushrooms, snap peas, ect)
  • Sauce (Soy Sauce, Rice Vinegar [or white vinegar], Sriracha, Mayo)

After gathering your ingredients, you have to move onto the next step...

Frying and Stirring

The next step towards making a successful stir fry is getting a wide and shallow pan. You want to ensure that your ingredients have as much contact as possible with your heat source. When cooking your stir fry, make sure to cook everything as flat as possible, which is to say DO NOT OVERLAP INGREDIENTS! It is far better to cook in several batches instead of overcrowding the pan with every ingredient all at once.

Cook your ingredients until they start to brown and caramelize. Don't overcook them, because your stir fry will burn. Nobody wants burnt stir fry! Add in any additional sauces or aromatics here.

Now that you've cooked your stir fry, you now need to worry about...

Serving your Stir Fry

Now, you can eat your stir fry as is, and it will taste great. To bring your stir fry to a different level, you need to focus on plating and side dishes. the most popular side dish with stir fry is rice. It works to balance out the intense and flavorful tastes that stir fry can have. To make your rice more exciting, you can always spice your rice by using coconut milk or whole peppercorns.

There are other alternatives to rice however. You can use stir fry as an addition to a salad or burrito. You can add stir fry to a packet of ramen to add some body and complexity to the humble dish. Ultimately, the sky is the limit when it comes to the applications that stir fry can be used in.

Now, go out there and make some stir fry for yourself!
